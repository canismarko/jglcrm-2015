Vernier Mini GC Plus Demonstration

This 30 minute demonstration will focus on the use of the Vernier Mini GC Plus gas chromatograph to investigate a forensics/CSI scenario. We will show how the Mini GC Plus measures and analyzes the components in a mixture of liquids.

Wireless Data Collection with Vernier Sensors

In this 30 minute presentation, you will have an opportunity to collect data wirelessly with Vernier sensors and the LabQuest 2 data collection interface. We will use Data Share, a free wi-fi browser option, to display collected sensor readings into any smart device. In addition, we will demonstrate wireless data collection with iPads and Vernier sensors.

Bringing Science to Life in the Classroom

  • Acids and Bases around the house ‐ use cabbage juice as an indicator or pH of common household substances.
  • Natural Acid/Base Indicators ‐ extract natural indicators from common plants and use to determine pH of common household substances.
  • Turmeric Paper ‐ make indicator paper using a spice which is a “litmus test” for bases.
  • Testing for Starch ‐ use tincture of iodine as an indicator for the presence of starch in food.
  • Red Lemons ‐ the old shell game.
  • Foam Fire Extinguisher ‐ using vinegar and baking soda.
  • States of Matter ‐ discover the properties of carbon dioxide and some of its uses.
  • A Balloon Full of Carbon Dioxide ‐ show how sublimation allows you to inflate a balloon with a solid.
  • A Glass of Carbon Dioxide ‐ Carbon dioxide displaces air in a glass.
  • Carbon Dioxide Fire Extinguisher ‐ use dry ice as a source to extinguish a candle.
  • Fun with Chromatography ‐ separate the pigments found in water-soluble markers.
  • Test for Oxygen ‐ produce oxygen from meat and hydrogen peroxide, and test for it.
  • Iron for Breakfast ‐ extract iron fillings with a magnet from “fortified” breakfast cereal.
  • Osmosis Through the membrane of an egg ‐ dissolve shell of a raw egg with vinegar, then explore the properties of the inner membrane using common solutions.
  • Evidence of Chemical Change ‐ study the interactions among a variety of chemical solutions to determine what constitutes evidence of chemical change.
  • Putter Putty ‐ demonstrate the properties of polymers using school glue and borax.
